# GEV vs ETN Relative Assessment v1

Date: March 22, 2026

## Purpose

Compare `GE Vernova` and `Eaton` as current public-market expressions of the
`grid equipment and transformer buildout` lane.

## Facts

### Market posture

`GEV`

1. `GEV` closed at `$877.39` on March 19, 2026 and sits only `1.96%` below its
   52-week high of `$894.93`.
2. Late-July 2025 price context was roughly `$624` to `$677`, so the move since
   then has been large but not a fresh multi-bagger from that date.
3. Market cap is roughly `$228B` to `$230B`.
4. TTM P/E is roughly `47x`.
5. One recent consensus source shows the average price target below the market
   price, implying the stock is no longer obviously under-owned on simple
   sell-side framing.

`ETN`

1. `ETN` closed at `$361.04` on March 16, 2026.
2. It remains `11.61%` below its 52-week high of `$408.45`.
3. Market cap is roughly `$138B`.
4. TTM P/E is roughly `34x`.
5. One recent consensus source shows about `11%` upside to the average target,
   so the stock looks less stretched than `GEV` on plain sell-side framing.

### Filing-backed structural evidence

`GEV`

1. Filing support is stronger and more bottleneck-like.
2. Local filing evidence counts:
   - `19` total evidence items
   - `16` strong
   - `6` component-specific
   - `5` pressure/capacity
   - `4` expansion/capex
3. The strongest filing excerpts are explicitly about:
   - transformers
   - substations
   - switchgear
   - supply-constrained operating conditions
   - factory capacity expansion

`ETN`

1. Filing support is real, but cleaner as beneficiary evidence than strict
   bottleneck evidence.
2. Local filing evidence counts:
   - `14` total evidence items
   - `11` strong
   - `2` component-specific
   - `6` pressure/capacity
   - `3` expansion/capex
3. The strongest filing excerpts are about:
   - transformer exposure
   - backlog around `$15.9B`
   - capacity expansion
4. The component specificity is weaker than `GEV`.

## Assessment

### What looks already priced

`GEV`

1. The market already recognizes that it is a high-quality grid beneficiary.
2. The stock is near its 52-week high.
3. The valuation multiple is richer than `ETN`.
4. The simplest rerating leg appears largely behind it.

`ETN`

1. Less of the pure transformer/grid bottleneck claim appears priced in.
2. The valuation is lower.
3. The stock is not sitting as close to its recent high.
4. The market seems to price it more as a broad electrical/platform winner than
   as a constrained-layer name.

### What may still be underappreciated

`GEV`

1. Duration of the grid bottleneck
2. Margin leverage if constrained components stay tight
3. Backlog and RPO conversion durability
4. Whether the market is still underestimating the second-order effect of grid
   modernization and data-center power demand on Grid Solutions

`ETN`

1. The materiality of transformer and adjacent electrical exposure inside the
   broader company
2. The degree to which backlog plus capacity buildout can still translate into
   another leg of earnings growth
3. Whether the market is still bundling too much of the story into generic
   electrification rather than constrained component leverage

## Decision

Best pure structural name:
1. `GEV`

Better candidate if the question is "what may still have more rerating room":
1. `ETN`

Best current framing:
1. `GEV` is the cleaner bottleneck thesis
2. `ETN` is the cleaner less-extended beneficiary thesis

## Practical implication

If capital is constrained to one name:
1. choose `GEV` for bottleneck purity
2. choose `ETN` for a less-stretched way to express the same infrastructure
   pressure complex

If both are kept:
1. `GEV` should carry the stricter bottleneck watch
2. `ETN` should carry the "underappreciated breadth and durability" watch
